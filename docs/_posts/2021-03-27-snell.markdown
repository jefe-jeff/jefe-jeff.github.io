snell's law relates the way that light bends when crossing between multiple uniform media to the speed of light in each medium. "Snell's Law of Sines" goes $$n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2$$

where $n_1, n_2$ are the refractive indices of the two media.

Fun question: Start by assuming $n_2 > n_1$. Choose some $\theta_1$. What happens to $\theta_2$ as we increase the ratio $n_1/n_2$? (Hint: Think about the complex nature of $\arcsin$)
